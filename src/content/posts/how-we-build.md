Every piece of cabinetry we install starts in our workshop in Thomastown. We do not build on site — we measure, design, construct, finish, and assemble in a controlled environment before delivering and installing in a single coordinated effort.

## Why off-site matters

Building in a workshop gives us consistent temperature, humidity, and dust control. That means:

- Paint and polyurethane finishes cure properly — no dust nibs, no uneven sheen
- Joints stay tight because the timber has acclimatised to the same conditions every day
- Every drawer, door, and panel is test-fitted before it leaves the shop
- Your home is not a construction site for weeks on end

## The process

1. **Measure** — We laser-measure and photograph every wall, corner, and service point.
2. **Design** — CAD drawings with your exact dimensions are reviewed and approved before any material is cut.
3. **Build** — Cabinetry is constructed in our workshop by cabinet makers who have been doing this for years.
4. **Finish** — Doors, panels, and carcases are sprayed, sanded, and sealed in our finishing bay.
5. **Install** — A crew delivers and installs everything in sequence, usually over a few days.
6. **Sign-off** — We walk through every opening and closing with you before we pack up.

It is not the fastest way to build cabinetry. But it is the right way.
